## Instructions

For discussions from a finance/business perspective, focus on:

### 1. Financial Impact
- **Cost Analysis**: Expenses, budgets, cost savings, ROI
- **Revenue Implications**: Pricing, monetization, revenue opportunities
- **Financial Metrics**: Burn rate, runway, unit economics, margins
- **Investment Decisions**: Capital allocation, resource investments

### 2. Business Model
- **Revenue Streams**: How money is made, pricing models
- **Cost Structure**: Fixed costs, variable costs, scaling economics
- **Profitability**: Path to profitability, break-even analysis
- **Business Viability**: Sustainability, financial risks

### 3. Budget & Resources
- **Budget Allocations**: Where money is going, spending priorities
- **Resource Planning**: Headcount, tools, infrastructure costs
- **Cost Optimization**: Efficiency improvements, cost reduction
- **Financial Forecasting**: Projections, scenarios, planning

### 4. Action Items (Finance-Focused)
- Financial models to build or update
- Budget proposals to create
- Cost analyses to perform
- Financial reports to generate
- Approval processes to initiate

### 5. Business Performance
- **KPIs & Metrics**: Revenue, costs, growth rates, conversion rates
- **Benchmarks**: Industry standards, competitive comparisons
- **Trends**: Financial trajectory, growth patterns
- **Variance Analysis**: Actuals vs. projections

### 6. Risk & Compliance
- Financial risks identified
- Compliance requirements
- Audit considerations
- Contractual obligations

### 7. Strategic Business Questions
- Pricing strategy and optimization
- Make vs. buy decisions
- Investment priorities
- Partnership economics
- Market expansion costs

## Output Format

Provide a summary that a finance professional could use to:
- Understand financial implications of decisions
- Assess ROI and business value
- Plan budgets and forecasts
- Identify cost optimization opportunities
- Make data-driven business recommendations

**Tone**: Analytical, data-driven, ROI-focused. Use financial and business terminology, emphasize quantifiable outcomes and business impact.
